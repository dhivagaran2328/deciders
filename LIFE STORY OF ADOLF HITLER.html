LIFE STORY OF ADOLF HITLER

Adolf Hitler was born on April 20, 1889, in Braunau am Inn, a small town in Austria. He grew up in a modest family and had a difficult relationship with his father, who was strict. As a young man, Hitler wanted to be an artist but was rejected twice by the Vienna Academy of Fine Arts.
** World War I**When World War I began in 1914, Hitler joined the German army and fought on the Western Front. He was a soldier who was wounded and awarded for bravery. The war had a big impact on him, and he became very upset when Germany lost in 1918.** Rise to Power**After the war, Germany was in chaos. The economy was terrible, and many people were unhappy with the government. Hitler joined a small political group called the German Workers’ Party, which later became the National Socialist German Workers’ Party (Nazi Party).Hitler was a powerful speaker and used his speeches to blame Germany’s problems on the Treaty of Versailles (which ended WWI) and on groups like Jews and Communists. He promised to restore Germany’s pride and fix the economy.In 1923, Hitler tried to seize power in Munich in what is called the “Beer Hall Putsch,” but it failed. He was arrested and spent nine months in prison, during which he wrote his book Mein Kampf ("My Struggle"), outlining his ideas about race and Germany’s future.Becoming Chancellor and DictatorIn the late 1920s and early 1930s, the Great Depression made Germany’s economy even worse. Many people turned to Hitler and the Nazi Party for hope. In 1933, Hitler was appointed Chancellor (the head of government).Once in power, Hitler quickly moved to make himself a dictator. He banned other political parties, controlled the media, and used propaganda to increase his control. The Nazis began persecuting Jews, political opponents, and other groups.** World War II**Hitler’s aggressive policies led to World War II. He wanted to expand Germany’s territory and created a military buildup. In 1939, Germany invaded Poland, starting the war.During the war, Hitler’s government committed terrible crimes, including the Holocaust, where six million Jews and millions of others were systematically murdered.** Defeat and Death**As the war turned against Germany, Hitler became more isolated. In April 1945, with Allied forces closing in on Berlin, Hitler committed suicide in his bunker.
